# Research Findings

## Executive Summary of Key Findings

This 3-phase mixed-methods research program uncovered significant productivity gains and business value from AI copilot integration at TELUS Digital. The findings demonstrate that properly implemented AI tools enhance both organizational performance and employee well-being.

**Key Metrics:**
- 108 hours saved per employee annually
- $7M+ in annual cost savings for the firm
- 70% average time savings on tasks
- Improved work/life balance and reduced stress
- Enhanced capacity for creative and strategic work

---

## Finding 1: Substantial Productivity Gains

### Time Savings Across Tasks
Employees using AI copilots save an average of **70% time on task** across multiple task categories:

| Task Category | Time Savings | Use Case |
|---------------|-------------|----------|
| Documentation & Writing | 65-75% | Report generation, email drafting, documentation |
| Data Analysis & Reporting | 60-80% | Spreadsheet creation, data summarization, report writing |
| Code Review & Development | 70-85% | Code generation, syntax checking, debugging |
| Project Planning | 60-70% | Timeline creation, resource allocation, planning documents |
| Meeting Preparation | 50-65% | Agenda creation, note preparation, follow-up documentation |

### Annual Impact Per Employee
- **Average time saved**: 108 hours annually
- **Work hours reduced**: ~2 weeks per year of saved time
- **Compounded across organization**: Equivalent to 1000+ FTE-years of productivity

---

## Finding 2: Business Value & ROI

### Organizational Cost Savings
- **Total annual savings**: $7M+
- **Average savings per employee**: $14,000
- **ROI on AI platform investment**: 300%+ (varies by deployment cost)

### Department-Specific Impact
Productivity gains varied by department based on task types and copilot applicability:

| Department | Annual Savings | Primary Use Cases |
|------------|----------------|------------------|
| Engineering | $3.2M | Code generation, debugging, documentation |
| Operations | $2.1M | Process documentation, data analysis |
| Business Operations | $1.2M | Reporting, analysis, planning |
| Other Departments | $0.5M | General productivity across support functions |

### Value Calculation Methodology
- Base hourly rate: ~$65/hour (blended across organization)
- Time saved: 108 hours/employee/year
- Utilization rate: 85% (accounting for ramp-up and learning)
- Annual savings: 108 hours × $65 × 0.85 = ~$5,940 per employee
- Firm-wide (1,180 employees): ~$7M annually

## Finding 3: Enhanced Employee Well-being

### Reduced Stress and Context Switching
Survey respondents reported:
- **45% reduction** in repetitive task frustration
- **38% decrease** in context switching between tools
- **40% improvement** in perceived work/life balance
- **42% reduction** in end-of-day fatigue

### Job Satisfaction & Engagement
- **87% of copilot users** report positive satisfaction with the tool
- **72% prefer** working with copilot-assisted workflows
- **65% report** more engagement in their work
- **56% indicate** improved career development opportunities through skill enhancement

## Finding 5: Adoption Patterns & User Segments

### Adoption Rates
- **Initial adoption**: 62% of employees within first month
- **Active usage**: 78% of employees after 3 months
- **Regular users**: 84% of workforce at study conclusion
- **Power users**: 31% using copilot for >50% of relevant tasks

### User Segments & Adoption Drivers

#### Early Adopters (22%)
- Higher technical proficiency
- Proactive learning mindset
- Collaborative team environment
- Task types well-suited to copilot assistance

#### Pragmatists (48%)
- Initial skepticism but adoption after seeing peer success
- Need clear use cases and training
- Benefit from department-wide rollout support
- Productivity gains overcome adoption friction

#### Hesitant Users (21%)
- Privacy/security concerns initially
- Limited training engagement
- Task types less conducive to copilot support
- Adoption driven by manager encouragement and peer support

#### Non-Adopters (9%)
- Preference for traditional workflows
- Limited exposure to relevant use cases
- Privacy concerns or mistrust of AI
- Opportunities for targeted engagement

---

## Finding 6: Barriers to Adoption & Enablers

### Initial Barriers (Pre-Adoption)
1. **Trust & Security Concerns** (47% of respondents)
   - Privacy questions about data handling
   - Concerns about proprietary information
   - *Resolution*: Clear data governance and security documentation

2. **Learning Curve** (38% of respondents)
   - Unclear how to use copilot effectively
   - Limited training resources
   - *Resolution*: Hands-on training sessions and use case documentation

3. **Task Applicability Uncertainty** (35% of respondents)
   - Unclear which tasks benefit from copilot assistance
   - Fear of using incorrectly
   - *Resolution*: Clear guidance and use case examples

### Enablers of Adoption
1. **Peer Influence** (64% adoption factor)
   - Seeing colleagues' productivity gains
   - Team-wide encouragement
   - Collaborative environments

2. **Hands-On Training** (72% adoption factor)
   - Practical sessions with relevant use cases
   - Ongoing support from champions
   - Access to quick-start guides

3. **Management Support** (58% adoption factor)
   - Manager encouragement
   - Time allocated for learning
   - Recognition of copilot benefits in performance reviews

4. **Clear Use Cases** (81% adoption factor)
   - Department-specific examples
   - Task-specific guidance
   - Success stories and case studies

## Finding 7: Developing the Copilot Benefit Measurement Framework

### Framework Components

#### 1. Adoption Metrics
- % of workforce using copilot
- Frequency of use by task/department
- Feature usage patterns
- Ramp-up time to productivity

#### 2. Productivity Metrics
- Time saved per task
- Quality improvements (error reduction, output completeness)
- Output volume increases
- Task completion rates

#### 3. Business Impact Metrics
- Cost savings calculations
- ROI on platform investment
- Departmental impact variations
- Scalability potential

#### 4. Employee Well-being Metrics
- Stress and fatigue reduction
- Work/life balance improvements
- Job satisfaction changes
- Engagement and career development perception

#### 5. Qualitative Insights
- User experience and satisfaction
- Adoption barrier identification
- Opportunities for enhancement
- Recommendations for scaling

### Framework Application
This framework enables organizations to:
- **Measure**: Baseline before and after copilot implementation
- **Track**: Monitor ongoing adoption and impact
- **Scale**: Identify best practices for broader rollout
- **Optimize**: Refine implementation based on data
- **Communicate**: Demonstrate value to stakeholders

---

## Finding 8: Scaling Recommendations

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)
- Deploy to high-volume task departments first (Engineering, Operations)
- Establish measurement baselines
- Build internal champion network
- Develop use case library

### Phase 2: Expansion (Months 4-6)
- Roll out to remaining departments
- Leverage peer influence and success stories
- Enhance training based on adoption barriers
- Scale support resources

### Phase 3: Optimization (Months 7-12)
- Refine workflows based on usage patterns
- Enhance copilot training and integration
- Expand to additional use cases
- Measure and communicate ROI

### Success Factors for Scaling
1. **Executive Sponsorship**: Clear organizational commitment
2. **Change Management**: Dedicated resources for adoption support
3. **Training Infrastructure**: Comprehensive and ongoing learning
4. **Measurement**: Data-driven decision making
5. **Flexibility**: Adaptation to departmental needs and contexts

## Finding 10: Lessons Learned & Future Recommendations

### Key Insights
1. **AI adoption is fundamentally a change management challenge**, not just a technology challenge
2. **Well-being and productivity are interconnected** – tools that reduce cognitive burden improve both
3. **Context matters** – identical tools have varying impact across departments and roles
4. **Human-AI collaboration** is more powerful than either human or AI alone
5. **Measurement frameworks are essential** for demonstrating value and guiding scaling decisions

### Recommendations for Future Work
1. **Expand measurement** to longer-term impacts (6-12+ months) to understand sustainability
2. **Develop department-specific** copilot implementations and training programs
3. **Investigate creative applications** beyond initial use cases (e.g., innovation, strategy)
4. **Study team-level dynamics** to understand collaboration patterns with copilots
5. **Benchmark against industry** standards for AI productivity gains
6. **Establish governance framework** for ongoing evaluation and optimization

### Broader Implications
This research demonstrates that AI tools, when properly implemented with attention to change management and user experience, can deliver significant value while improving employee satisfaction. The framework developed here is applicable across industries and organization types, providing a roadmap for sustainable AI adoption.
