# Research Methodology

## Overview

This 3-phase mixed-methods research program was designed to comprehensively evaluate TELUS Digital's internally built AI platform and quantify its impact on employee productivity, well-being, and organizational value.

## Research Design

### Mixed-Methods Approach

The study combined both qualitative and quantitative research methods to provide:
- **Depth**: Understanding *why* employees use or don't use AI copilots
- **Breadth**: Measuring *how much* impact copilots have across the organization
- **Context**: Real-world workflow insights and behavioral data

## Phase 1: Discovery & Baseline (Weeks 1-4)

### Objectives
- Understand current workflows and pain points
- Identify opportunities for AI copilot integration
- Establish baseline productivity and employee sentiment metrics
- Map employee personas and use cases

### Methods
- **Qualitative Interviews**: Semi-structured interviews with 20-30 employees across different departments and roles
- **Focus Groups**: 3-4 focus groups exploring specific workflow challenges
- **Workflow Mapping**: Detailed analysis of current processes and time allocations
- **Platform Usage Analytics**: Baseline data on current tool adoption and usage patterns

### Key Outputs
- Employee journey maps
- Workflow analysis and bottleneck identification
- Baseline productivity metrics
- Employee sentiment baseline
- Use case prioritization matrix

### Tools Used
- UserInterviews (participant recruitment)
- Figma (journey mapping and visualizations)
- Qualtrics (baseline survey)

---

## Phase 2: Evaluation & Usability Testing (Weeks 5-12)

### Objectives
- Evaluate AI copilot usability and user experience
- Test effectiveness across different workflows
- Identify adoption barriers and enablers
- Gather detailed feedback on features and functionality

### Methods
- **Usability Testing**: 8-10 moderated sessions with target users
- **A/B Testing**: Comparative analysis of copilot-assisted vs. traditional workflows
- **Task Analysis**: Detailed measurement of time on task, task success rates, and error rates
- **In-app Behavioral Tracking**: HotJar heatmaps and interaction tracking
- **Comparative Workflow Trials**: Side-by-side testing of standard vs. AI-assisted processes

### Metrics Captured
- Task completion time (with and without copilot)
- Error rates and quality of outputs
- User satisfaction and ease-of-use scores
- Feature usage and adoption rates
- Context switching frequency
- Perceived stress levels during tasks

### Key Outputs
- Usability findings and recommendations
- Task time savings documentation
- User experience insights
- Feature prioritization based on user feedback
- Adoption barrier identification

### Tools Used
- UserInterviews (participant recruitment)
- Qualtrics (task feedback and surveys)
- HotJar (behavioral tracking)
- Figma (findings visualization)
- Google Suite (analysis and reporting)

---

## Phase 3: Impact Measurement & Framework Development (Weeks 13-20)

### Objectives
- Quantify productivity and business value gains
- Develop a framework for measuring copilot benefits
- Identify scaling recommendations
- Create implementation roadmap

### Methods
- **Large-Scale Quantitative Survey**: 200+ employee survey measuring productivity changes, well-being, and value perception
- **Behavioral Data Analysis**: Analysis of copilot usage patterns and productivity metrics across the organization
- **Business Impact Modeling**: ROI calculations based on time savings and organizational value
- **Statistical Analysis**: Correlation analysis between copilot usage and productivity improvements
- **Qualitative Validation**: Follow-up interviews to validate quantitative findings

### Key Metrics
- **Productivity Gains**:
  - Average time saved per employee (annually)
  - Time savings by task type and department
  - Quality improvements and error reduction
  
- **Employee Well-being**:
  - Work/life balance improvements
  - Stress reduction metrics
  - Job satisfaction changes
  - Context switching reduction
  
- **Business Value**:
  - Total annual cost savings
  - Return on investment (ROI)
  - Departmental and role-based impact variations

### Framework Development
Created a replicable framework for organizations to:
- Measure copilot adoption and usage
- Quantify productivity gains
- Calculate business value and ROI
- Identify opportunities for scaling
- Benchmark performance across teams

### Key Outputs
- Comprehensive impact report with statistical analysis
- Business case and ROI documentation
- Copilot Benefit Measurement Framework
- Scaling recommendations by department and role
- Implementation roadmap for broader adoption

### Tools Used
- Qualtrics (large-scale survey)
- Looker (data visualization and dashboards)
- Google Suite (statistical analysis)
- Figma (report design and visualizations)

## Data Collection Methods

### Surveys
- **Baseline Survey** (Phase 1): Establish starting metrics
- **Usability Surveys** (Phase 2): Real-time feedback on copilot experience
- **Impact Survey** (Phase 3): Large-scale measurement of productivity and well-being changes

### Interviews & Focus Groups
- **Discovery Interviews**: Deep dives into current workflows and pain points
- **Usability Testing Sessions**: Think-aloud protocols during copilot usage
- **Validation Interviews**: Confirmation of quantitative findings with qualitative context

### Behavioral Data
- **Platform Analytics**: Usage patterns, feature adoption, engagement metrics
- **Task Metrics**: Time on task, completion rates, error rates
- **Interaction Tracking**: User journey mapping through HotJar

---

## Participant Recruitment

- **Total Participants**: Full TELUS Digital employee base
- **Recruitment Channels**: 
  - UserInterviews platform for phase 1 & 2 participants
  - Direct email survey distribution for phase 3
  - Department-specific outreach
  
- **Sampling Strategy**: 
  - Stratified sampling across departments and role types
  - Representation of both early adopters and hesitant users
  - Mix of experienced and new employees

---

## Analysis & Synthesis

### Qualitative Analysis
- Thematic coding of interview and focus group transcripts
- Pattern identification across user segments
- Journey mapping and persona development
- Workflow bottleneck analysis

### Quantitative Analysis
- Descriptive statistics (means, medians, distributions)
- Inferential statistics (t-tests, correlation analysis, regression)
- Time series analysis of adoption trends
- ROI and business impact calculations

### Mixed-Methods Integration
- Convergent design: Qual findings inform quantitative measures and vice versa
- Sequential design: Phase outputs inform subsequent phase design
- Triangulation: Multiple data sources validate findings

## Quality Assurance

- **Participant Consent**: All participants provided informed consent
- **Data Security**: Anonymized data with secure storage and access controls
- **Inter-rater Reliability**: Multiple coders reviewed qualitative data
- **Validity Checks**: Triangulation across multiple data sources
- **Ethical Considerations**: Neutral moderation, no leading questions, participant confidentiality

---

## Limitations

- Study focused on TELUS Digital's specific AI platform implementation
- Generalization to other organizations may require context-specific adaptation
- Findings reflect timeframe of study; ongoing usage patterns may evolve
- Adoption influenced by organizational support and training factors
